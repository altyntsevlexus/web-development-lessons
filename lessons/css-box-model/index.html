Jupiter: The Giant Planet

Jupiter is the largest planet in the Solar System. Its famous Great Red Spot is a storm that has been raging for over 350 years. One day on Jupiter lasts only 10 hours.

Dr. Anna Kowalski

Astrophysicist

Did you know?

Light from the Sun takes approximately 8 minutes and 20 seconds to reach Earth. When you look at the Sun, you are seeing it as it was over 8 minutes ago.

The Solar System

The Solar System formed 4.6 billion years ago from the gravitational collapse of a giant interstellar molecular cloud.

The vast majority of the system's mass is in the Sun, with most of the remaining mass contained in the planet Jupiter.

The four inner planets — Mercury, Venus, Earth and Mars — are terrestrial planets, composed primarily of rock and metal.

Beyond the planets, the Solar System is also home to regions populated by smaller objects, including the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter.
